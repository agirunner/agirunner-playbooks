# Business Case Structuring

## Purpose
Move from “should we do this?” to a decision-ready comparison of options.

## Use When
- leadership needs a recommendation with tradeoffs
- a project, vendor, or process change needs justification
- evidence exists but is not yet structured for a decision

## Do
- Define the decision to be made.
- Compare plausible options, including the status quo.
- Quantify impact where the evidence supports it.
- Separate facts, assumptions, and open questions.
- End with a recommendation tied to the stated decision.

## Do Not
- Hide assumptions inside claimed facts.
- Present only one option unless the alternatives are truly non-viable.
- Overstate precision when the evidence is directional.

## Output
- `decision`
- `options`
- `evaluation_criteria`
- `evidence_and_assumptions`
- `recommended_option`
- `confidence`
