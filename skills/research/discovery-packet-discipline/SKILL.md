# Discovery Packet Discipline

## Purpose
Turn a discovery brief into a bounded packet of relevant candidates,
signals, sources, or entities without hiding weak evidence or open gaps.

## Use When
- the workflow starts from a discovery brief instead of a single known answer
- the output should rank or group candidates, sources, or market entities
- operators need evidence-backed next steps, not a bag of links

## Do
- Restate the discovery brief, scope, and inclusion rules before searching.
- Prefer direct, recent, and sourceable evidence over weak aggregation.
- Remove weak candidates instead of padding the result set.
- Explain why each included candidate matters to the brief.
- Rank or group findings using one consistent comparison frame.
- Mark where confidence is high, medium, or weak.
- When the evidence is thin, narrow the packet and say what remains missing.

## Do Not
- Treat generic listicles or low-signal mentions as strong inclusion proof.
- Mix confirmed candidates with speculative ones without labeling them.
- Inflate a shortlist just because the brief asked for more items.
- Hide search gaps behind polished language.

## Output
- `discovery_brief`
- `search_scope`
- `ranked_findings`
- `supporting_evidence`
- `gaps_or_uncertainty`
- `recommended_next_step`
